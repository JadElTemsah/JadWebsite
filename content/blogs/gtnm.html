Introduction

My name is Jad El Temsah, and I am London Business School MFA student. I was born and raised in Beirut , Lebanon. I have always tried to get out of my comfort zone by exploring new things which led me to my passion in finance.

Exploring My Horizon

As I explored new activities to do, I stumbled upon sports, many of them. My involvement in sports developed my competitive nature and forged my Why can’t I be the best mentality. Playing certain type of sports forces a player to pick up traits they didn’t have before. Being in my high school football team definitely taught me how to be a team player by acknowledging my role and accepting that I was part of a unit that needed me to excel in my role and trust others to reach our goal. There are a handful of people I would call my role model such as:

  • Parents
  • Warren Buffet
  • Muhammad Ali
  • Andrew Carnegie

As you could see Muhammad Ali is one of them, His charisma and confidence left a mark in me that I still adopt in my time of need to this day. I decided to challenge myself by starting boxing training, and it sure was a challenge. Going from football into boxing was a drastic change, it was completely opposite to what I’m used to. This time I was alone in the ring with nothing except my training and my mindset. I discovered where Muhammad Ali got his confidence, with all the training in the world if I didn’t believe I am going to win, I won’t. Moreover, being an individual sport, boxing forced me to be depend on myself and stay on my toes or to accept that a loss would come in a form of a punch. Believing in myself combined with my awareness of my competencies made me relentlessly find efficient and effective ways to improve. The foundation to my mindset is belief, you can call it whatever you want, but without knowing and believing that you are able to finish the task at hand, you simply won’t. Being self-aware is a must and it goes hand in hand with my belief. It works as a reality check. I know I could be the best, but its easier to live in bubble and lie to myself that I’m already the best and that’s where self-awareness comes in hand. The reason why I chose “exploring” instead of “explored” is because I am going to keep on exploring for the rest of my life, knowing that the horizon is limitless.

Finding my Passion

On the journey to exploring my horizon, there are a lot of things that I liked but few things that I really loved, finance was one of them. I found a lot of similarities between me and finance, always trying to improve an inefficient and effective manner. I used my belief and confidence that I picked up from Mohammad Ali in my school when they decided for me that I should pursue biology and premed. I remember carrying my desk to the economics class and telling them that I will pursue what I love only. I was immediately called to the principal’s office and deep in my heart I thought that I was going to be suspended but after passionately speaking my hearts out about finance and economics, He decided to enroll me in the class. That confrontation was a steppingstone for me to pursue a Finance degree in the best university in the region, pass my CFA level 1 and currently pursue an MFA in London Business School, knowing that I’m moving towards my horizon.
